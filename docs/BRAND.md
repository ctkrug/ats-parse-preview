# Brand

The positioning decisions the README, the page copy, and the launch posts all key off. Change
this file first if any of them should change.

## Audience brief

**Who:** someone forty applications into a job search who has heard nothing back, and has
started to suspect the problem is not their experience but the tidy two-column template they
downloaded. They are technical enough to open a network tab if you tell them to, and cynical
enough to close any page that asks for their email first.

**The one pain:** they cannot see what the machine sees. The resume looks perfect on screen,
the rejections are silent and unexplained, and every tool that claims to help wants an email
address in exchange for a number out of 100 that explains nothing.

**The single core benefit:** the exact text an ATS pulls out of their file, on screen in about
four seconds, without handing anything to anyone.

## Name

**Readout.** An instrument gives a readout, and this is an instrument: it reports what was read
out of your file and refuses to grade it. Two syllables, spelled the way it sounds, and it sits
with the blueprint direction in `DESIGN.md` rather than fighting it.

The working title "ATS Parse Preview" was a description of the feature, not a name. It is three
unstressed words, it cannot be said out loud without sounding like a menu item, and it puts the
jargon ("ATS", "parse") ahead of the payoff.

**Tagline:** See what an ATS reads. No email.

## Voice

Flat and specific. The product's whole claim is that it does not oversell, so the copy cannot
either: every sentence names a real capability, a real number, or a real failure it catches.
Second person, short sentences, no exclamation marks.

Concretely, the copy does not use: em-dashes, "seamless", "effortless", "unlock", "empower",
"robust", "leverage", or any superlative that is not backed by something a reader can check in
the tool itself. If a sentence could appear on any other resume tool, it gets rewritten until
it could not.
